Investors put money into a company in exchange for stock, but their stock is "special" due to terms such as "liquidation preference" and "participation". What this boils down to is that, in the case of an acquisition or IPO, the investor usually gets a big take of the money before any of the other stock holders. You'd have to read all the term sheets for the company and do lots of math, so for this simplified calculation, try to estimate it with an average "investor multiplier". Somewhere between 1x and 10x is probably a reasonable guess.
